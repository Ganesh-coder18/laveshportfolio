Description

ML based Indian Unconventional Emission Prediction System For Indian Scenario.

The purpose of this project is to analyze and find the most optimal ML algorithm which can be used to build real time conventional and unconventional intersection designs which will help reduce the fuel consumption, CO2, NOX, Emissions etc. since it provides free flow turns while entering and exiting an interstate. This system will help in designing roads while will substitute the traditional urban road system and it will decrease the traffic jams and avoid most accidents as a part of road safety. It will also help in reducing fuel efficiency, emissions to control air quality of vehicle using the road.

In our project, we developed IUEPS (Indian Unconventional Emission Prediction System) for both unconventional (Divering Diamond Interchange: Bhandup, Lonavala and Andheri) and conventional scenarios (Four way intersection: Vashi-Belapur) on identified Indian locations for simulating and generating data, assisting in developing a prediction model. An unconventioanl scenario is a road or highway that deviates from the standard or traditional design principles it may include innovative features, alternative layouts, or unique design elements that challenge the conventional norms of road construction. The purpose of unconventional roadways is often to address specific challenges, improve safety, enhance traffic flow, or provide unique aesthetic or functional benefits. In contrast, conventional scenario is a road or highway that follows standard design principles and conforms to established norms and guidelines. Conventional roadways are typically designed and constructed based on well-established engineering practices and standards to ensure safety, efficiency, and compatibility with other transportation infrastructure.

As part of our research, we used applications like PTV-Vissim and SUMO3 for simulating roads like diverging diamond interchanges (DDI), complex intersections, and R-cuts in order to generate data. In PTV-Vissim, we simulated different scenarios for 3600sec with 500 vehicles on average, while in SUMO3, we simulated scenarios for 600sec with 500 vehicles on average.
